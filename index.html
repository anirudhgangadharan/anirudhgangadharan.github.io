I am a medical student–researcher working at the interface of clinical medicine, artificial intelligence, and biomedical innovation. My work focuses on developing and validating AI-driven diagnostic systems that translate complex R&D into practical tools for real-world care. I have developed and am currently validating a smartphone-based hemoglobin estimation pipeline in a cohort of 600 pregnant women, aimed at scalable anemia screening in resource-limited settings. In parallel, I am collecting data for a computer vision–based refractive error screening system. More broadly, I am interested in leveraging synthetic biology and programmable biological systems to design robust, low-cost solutions for under-resourced and rural healthcare environments. My long-term research direction integrates clinical insight, machine learning, and neurotechnology, with a particular interest in transformer-based brain–computer interfaces and engineered biological substrates capable of bidirectional interaction with human neural tissue. The overarching goal is to build deployable, high-impact technologies that extend diagnostic capacity and enable next-generation neural interfacing.
